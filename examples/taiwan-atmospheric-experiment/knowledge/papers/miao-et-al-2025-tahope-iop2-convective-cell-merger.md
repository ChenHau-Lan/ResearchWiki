## Reading Notes

- Research question: How do merged and isolated convective cells differ in
  microphysical and kinematic structure over the complex terrain of the Taipei
  Basin?
- Data used: NCAR S-Pol radar, multi-Doppler winds, polarimetric signatures,
  range-height indicator scans, and TAHOPE/PRECIP event observations.
- Core finding: Multiple cell merger favored by terrain-induced circulation was
  associated with stronger updrafts, broader enhanced ZDR regions, graupel
  growth, and extreme rainfall.
- Method value for RKF: TAHOPE adds a modern storm-microphysics layer to the
  Taiwan field-campaign lineage: high-time-resolution dual-pol radar plus
  multi-Doppler retrievals.

## Claims To Promote

- Claim: Future Taiwan severe-rainfall experiments should include polarimetric
  radar scan strategies capable of resolving ZDR-column evolution during cell
  mergers over complex terrain.
  - PDF locator: pp. 1-2 and pp. 7-14.
  - Caveat: The paper focuses on Taipei Basin afternoon thunderstorms; compare
    with Mei-yu frontal and typhoon cases before generalizing.
