## Reading Notes

- Research question: What was the structure and evolution of the midlevel vortex
  observed during TAMEX IOP 10?
- Data used: surface and upper-air observations, conventional and Doppler radar,
  NOAA P-3 aircraft measurements, and enhanced GMS infrared imagery.
- Core finding: The case links mesoscale vortex structure, terrain lifting, and
  northeastern Taiwan heavy rainfall, showing why Taiwan campaigns need
  coordinated multi-platform observations.
- Method value for RKF: The paper is a good template for how to write a field
  experiment note: identify the observing network, event window, target
  phenomenon, and mechanism chain.

## Claims To Promote

- Claim: Taiwan terrain-rainfall experiments need simultaneous radar, aircraft,
  surface, and upper-air observations to resolve mesoscale vortex evolution.
  - PDF locator: pp. 25-26 observing systems and analysis scope.
  - Caveat: This is one TAMEX case study; compare with other IOPs before
    generalizing.
