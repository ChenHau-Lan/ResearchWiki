# Chang et al. 2021: CCN And Diurnal Precipitation Over Taiwan Topography

## Source Identity

- DOI: `10.5194/acp-21-16709-2021`
- Journal: Atmospheric Chemistry and Physics
- Region: Taiwan complex topography
- Evidence status: QCed open-access publisher PDF

## Reading Notes

- Research question: How can CCN perturbations affect summer diurnal
  precipitation systems over Taiwan's complex terrain?
- Method: Semi-realistic LES experiments with TaiwanVVM and object-based
  tracking of precipitating systems.
- Core finding: Under weak synoptic regimes, CCN changes can affect timing,
  organization, and extremes of orographically driven diurnal convection.
- Method value for RKF: Observation campaigns should preserve the life cycle of
  convective systems, not only event totals. Tracking and timing matter.

## Claims To Promote

- Claim: Future Taiwan experiments should measure aerosol and local circulation
  states together because terrain-locked convection can respond to both.
  - PDF locator: pp. 16709-16711 abstract and topography/convective tracking
    rationale.
  - Caveat: This paper is simulation-focused; pair with in-situ observational
    studies before turning it into operational design rules.
