# Lin et al. 2026: Lulin Aerosol-Cloud Mixing Ratio

## Source Identity

- DOI: `10.5194/acp-26-2083-2026`
- Journal: Atmospheric Chemistry and Physics
- Site: Lulin Atmospheric Background Station, central Taiwan
- Observation context: 2024 spring absorbing-aerosol and warm-cloud events
- Evidence status: QCed open-access publisher PDF

## PDF Locators

- ACP PDF pp. 2083-2084: title, abstract, Lulin 2024 observing context.
- ACP PDF pp. 2097-2099: Twomey vs semi-direct regimes and data availability.

## Reading Notes

- Research question: How can cloud-aerosol mixing ratio help separate Twomey
  and semi-direct effects in absorbing aerosol environments?
- Data used: high-time-resolution in-situ aerosol/cloud microphysics at LABS,
  lidar, PM2.5, liquid water content, and droplet number/size metrics.
- Core finding: Aerosol-cloud responses can change sign depending on the
  relationship between aerosol loading and liquid water; paired aerosol and
  cloud-water observations are therefore crucial.
- Method value for RKF: Future Taiwan field campaigns should treat in-cloud
  microphysics, aerosol absorption, humidity, and liquid water as one coupled
  measurement problem.

## Claims To Promote

- Claim: A future Taiwan aerosol-cloud campaign should include in-situ cloud
  droplet measurements and liquid-water constraints, not only aerosol loading.
  - PDF locator: pp. 2083-2084 and pp. 2097-2099.
  - Caveat: The paper is focused on absorbing aerosol and warm-cloud events at
    LABS; extend with other sites and seasons.
