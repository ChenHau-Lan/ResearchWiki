# Observation-To-Prediction Campaign Design

## Definition

A field-campaign design pattern where instruments, intensive observation
periods, case catalogs, data assimilation, nowcasting, and model evaluation are
planned together from the start.

## Why It Matters For Taiwan

Taiwan heavy-rainfall experiments are not only about collecting dense
observations. They also need to test whether observations improve warning,
QPE/QPF, radar assimilation, and microphysics representation over steep terrain
and coastal cities.

## Evidence Links

- You et al. 2020 shows that SoWMEX IOP8 radar observations can diagnose model
  microphysics and observation-operator behavior.
- Miao et al. 2025 shows that TAHOPE/PRECIP high-resolution radar observations
  can resolve storm-merger and microphysical signatures relevant to extreme
  rainfall prediction.
- The TAHOPE project overview frames real-time or near-real-time data
  assimilation as part of the experiment objective.

## Design Implication

Future campaigns should pre-register forecast products, evaluation metrics,
case IDs, data latency targets, and evidence locators so that field operations
and downstream wiki synthesis reinforce each other.
