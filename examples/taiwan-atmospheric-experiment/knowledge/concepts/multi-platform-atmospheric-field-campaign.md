# Multi-Platform Atmospheric Field Campaign

## Definition

A field campaign design that measures the same atmospheric event from multiple
platforms and scales: surface network, radar, upper-air soundings, aircraft or
UAV transects, satellite imagery, aerosol chemistry, CCN spectra, lidar,
disdrometers, wind profilers, dropsondes, data-assimilation products, and
in-cloud microphysics.

## Why It Matters For Taiwan

Taiwan's terrain and monsoon environment create rapid transitions among
upstream flow, windward lifting, lee-side response, coastal/local circulation,
and mountain convection. A single platform rarely captures the whole mechanism
chain.

## Evidence Links

- Chen and Liang 1992 shows a TAMEX case where surface, upper-air, radar,
  aircraft, and satellite evidence were needed together.
- You et al. 2020 shows how SoWMEX dual-polarimetric radar can connect field
  observations with model microphysics evaluation.
- Miao et al. 2025 shows how TAHOPE/PRECIP radar and multi-Doppler winds can
  resolve convective cell merger and microphysical intensification.
- The TAHOPE project overview shows that operational facilities, international
  research instruments, and near-real-time data assimilation should be planned
  as one campaign system.
- Cheung et al. 2020 shows why aerosol chemistry, size distribution, CCN, and
  air-mass history should be paired.
- Lin et al. 2026 shows why in-cloud microphysics and aerosol/cloud-water
  constraints matter for aerosol-cloud interpretation.
