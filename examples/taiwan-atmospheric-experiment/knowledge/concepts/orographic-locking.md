# Orographic Locking

## Definition

Orographic locking is the tendency for convective initiation, organization, or
rainfall maxima to remain tied to terrain-forced circulations or lifting zones.

## Why It Matters For Taiwan

Taiwan's Central Mountain Range can organize precipitation systems and alter
their timing, intensity, and movement. A future campaign should therefore
sample event life cycle and local circulation, not only rain totals.

## Evidence Links

- Chang et al. 2021 frames Taiwan summer diurnal precipitation as a
  complex-terrain problem where tracking convective objects is useful.
- Chen and Liang 1992 links terrain lifting and vortex structure to heavy
  rainfall in a TAMEX case.
