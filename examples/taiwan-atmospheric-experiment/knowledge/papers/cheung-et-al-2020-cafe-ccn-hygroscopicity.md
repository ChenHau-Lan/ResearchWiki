# Cheung et al. 2020: Cape Fuguei CCN Hygroscopicity

## Source Identity

- DOI: `10.5194/acp-20-5911-2020`
- Journal: Atmospheric Chemistry and Physics
- Site: Cape Fuguei Research Station, northern Taiwan
- Observation period: April 2017 to March 2018
- Evidence status: checked open-access publisher PDF

## PDF Locators

- ACP PDF pp. 5911-5912: abstract, motivation, Taiwan downwind setting.
- ACP PDF pp. 5912-5913: Cape Fuguei site and instrumentation.

## Reading Notes

- Research question: How do continental outflow, local pollution, and new
  particle formation affect aerosol hygroscopicity and CCN activity at a Taiwan
  coastal site?
- Data used: PM2.5 chemistry, aerosol number and size distributions, CCN
  number concentration, back-trajectory clustering, and site instrumentation.
- Core finding: Taiwan's location downstream of East Asian outflow makes it a
  useful natural laboratory for CCN activation under mixed regional and local
  pollution influences.
- Method value for RKF: A future campaign should pair aerosol chemistry, size
  distribution, CCN spectra, trajectory analysis, and meteorological context.

## Claims To Promote

- Claim: Taiwan aerosol-cloud observation design needs both aerosol chemistry
  and CCN measurements because source-region changes alter activation behavior.
  - PDF locator: pp. 5911-5913 abstract and site/instrumentation sections.
  - Caveat: This is a coastal site; mountain and in-cloud regimes need separate
    observation pages.
